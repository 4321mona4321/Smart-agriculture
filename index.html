Introduction

As the global population approaches 9.73 billion by 2050, the demand for agricultural products continues to rise. To meet this challenge, Smart Agriculture is revolutionizing traditional farming practices. Leveraging advanced technologies such as the Internet of Things (IoT), Artificial Intelligence (AI), and Machine Learning (ML), Smart Agriculture aims to enhance productivity and precise management in food production.

Remote Sensing and Statistics: Utilizing satellite imagery and mapping to optimize resource allocation, especially in arid climates.

ML Approaches for Precise Agricultural Mapping: Applying machine learning techniques to create accurate agricultural maps.

IoT-Driven Agricultural Management: Using IoT devices for real-time monitoring and decision-making on farms.

Cloud-Based Satellite Systems for Irrigation Mapping: Enhancing irrigation efficiency through cloud-based satellite data.

Decision Support Systems (DSS): Assisting farmers in making informed choices about crop rotation, resource allocation, and environmental impact.
